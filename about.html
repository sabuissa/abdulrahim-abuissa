About

I'm a Qatari entrepreneur, investor, professional basketball player, and social impact leader. Shaped by years of international education in the United States, I've built a career at the intersection of business, sport, and community development — grounded in clear communication, disciplined execution, and a commitment to long-term impact.

As the first Qatari to compete at the NCAA Division I level, I've carried that experience into building sport and community in Qatar — as founder of Abuissa Sports, as a FIBA Foundation Youth Leader, and as an investor in ventures aligned with innovation and opportunity.

Education

Williston Northampton School — Prep school in the United States, where I was a Northeast Champion in the 110m hurdles and received the Denman Award as Athlete of the Year.

Sacred Heart University — Competed at the NCAA Division I level, contributing to a historic 21-win season and representing Qatar on the national stage.

Leadership

My approach to leadership is rooted in accountability, clear communication, and a long-term view of impact. Whether building a business, developing athletes, or running community programs, I lead with the belief that sustained effort and genuine relationships create the most durable outcomes.

I've had the opportunity to lead organizations, represent Qatar on international platforms, and work directly with young people across diverse backgrounds — and each experience has reinforced that leadership is less about position and more about consistent, purposeful action.
