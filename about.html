About

I'm a professional basketball player and entrepreneur, proud to be the first Qatari to play NCAA Division 1 basketball. Alongside representing Qatar's National Team, I lead Abuissa Sports and recently launched the country's first fully Qatari-owned basketball academy.

My work blends sport, business, and community impact — from hosting elite training camps with NBA coaches to driving grassroots programs that bring kids together on and off the court.

On the Court

As the first Qatari to compete at the NCAA Division 1 level, I've carried that experience back home as a member of Qatar's National Basketball Team. Representing my country at the highest level shapes everything I do — it's the foundation for the work I now lead off the court.

Abuissa Sports

I founded Abuissa Sports to grow the game in Qatar the right way. We launched the country's first fully Qatari-owned and operated basketball academy, bringing NBA-standard coaching to young athletes — including elite training camps led by international and NBA coaches.

Our programs have reached hundreds of athletes across Qatar, building not just skills but mental strength, teamwork, and a lasting love for the game.

Community Impact

Sport is a tool for change. As a FIBA Foundation Youth Leader, I led the Hoops For All Camp, which brought together over 135 participants from 18 nationalities — including 50 young people evacuated from Gaza — to promote integration and unity through basketball, in partnership with Generation Amazing, the Qatar Basketball Federation, and the FIBA Foundation.

From charity camps for underprivileged kids to grassroots programs across the country, I'm committed to using basketball to bring communities together and create opportunities for the next generation.
